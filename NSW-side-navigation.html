Rediscovering the NSW Side Navigation

  • By Andrei Vidaicu
  • August 28, 2023

After assessing forty plus websites that adopt the NSW Design System, many of them have up to five levels of site hierarchy. It was clear that we needed to improve on the current side navigation component.

Context

The initial phase aimed to enhance the existing static version, while also considering a future update that would introduce expand and collapse functionality.

I created an open space for collaborating with our design system users on the Digital NSW community website.

https://community.digital.nsw.gov.au/t/collab-space-side-navigation-v2/1484

Feedback received included
  • As subsections grew users found the indent hard to follow
  • As subsections grew the red highlight lost it’s value and no longer aided with hierarchy
  • Users found the UI cluttered and hard to group content

Research

A heuristics evaluation revealed some key usability issues that we would later evaluate through usability testing.

We also explored a range of other leading government side navigations, identifying strengths and weaknesses, which gave valuable insights into emerging trends and best practices.

This analysis helped create the foundations of the crucial areas to focus on moving forward.

Exploration

Regular design reviews involving designers, developers, and stakeholders helped refine the design iteratively.

Evaluation

Conducted usability testing with a diverse group of users.

Key takeaways
  • Non active main category and category relationship is hard to establish when categories appeared at the bottom of the page (below the fold).
  • Level 2 items were inconsistent with the other levels.
  • At first glance it's not easy to distinguish the nested levels of hierarchy.
https://miro.com/app/board/uXjVM3T7iXk=/?share_link_id=266903587044

Implementation and documentation

Futher design iterations were made based on the testing. Communication and documentation played a vital role in this phase to kick off the dev build. Worked closely with the developers providing detailed usage and guidelines, and interactive examples using our recent 'Component Spec Library'.

https://www.figma.com/file/dHW0Cos6ldvx7Q1cVrdAcz/NSW-DDS-Component-Spec-Library?type=design&node-id=5%3A2675&mode=design&t=zKMc7eBMnM3WYOzS-1

Learnings and takeaways

A pivotal part in the success of the side navigation component have been around the importance of collaboration - involving stakeholders, cross functional teams, our design system partners and different product design teams early on.

Testing our assumptions through usability testing has not only evaluated that the design is working for our users, but it also revealed additional insights, improving it beyond the goals that we set out to achieve.
