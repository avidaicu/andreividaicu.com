From Art and Animals to Pixels and Code

It all started with a passion for art and computers.

As a young child, I was captivated by my grandfather’s artistry, transforming each letter of the alphabet into a playful animal shape. His creative influence inspired my major work in art class during my last year of school.

My love for computers began with video games, though they used to crash and freeze back then – not so fun! But hey, fixing them was strangely satisfying.

Bridging the Gap Between Art and Technology

The merging of art and computers taught me valuable life skills: creativity, perseverance, and a deep desire to understand systems and interfaces. This combination of art and computers, ultimately led me to pursue a career in coding and design. After finishing an Advanced Diploma of Graphic Design in Visual Communications in the early 2000s, I embarked on a journey as a print designer both in Sydney and abroad.

The Digital Design Adventure Begins

Around 2009, I took my first foray into digital design – thanks to my helpful developer friend. Together, we launched FindaTeammate.com.au, a platform to connect basketball players in our local area. The countless design iterations!! While I relied on my instincts and best practices, it provided a valuable lesson in understanding how websites are built, how they break, and the intricacies of SEO.

It was a learning curve, but it ultimately paved the way into digital design and coding.

Elevating User Experiences with the NSW Design System

Fast forward to the present, and my focus lies in systems thinking, promoting efficiency and cohesion, and emphasizing on accessibility standards and compliance. In my current role as a Senior UX/UI Designer, I work on the NSW Design System – collaborating with teams to deliver exceptional user experiences to the people of NSW.
